ISOM 835 | PREDICTIVE ANALYTICS

Forecasting the U.S. Housing Market

An Examination of Macro-Economic Determinants using Gradient Boosting Architectures.

Executive Research Objectives

This project investigates the relationship between Federal Monetary Policy and the S&P Case-Shiller Home Price Index. By utilizing primary source data from the Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) repository, we established a predictive framework to forecast housing price volatility through a 3-month temporal lag.

Critical Insights & Analytical Findings

"By achieving an R² of 0.7019, this research quantifies a 70% 'Macro-Stability Anchor' where housing prices are dictated by federal intervention."

The remaining 30% of unexplained variance highlights the influence of Hyper-Local variables. This includes municipal zoning restrictions, regional migration patterns, and inventory shortages in high-growth corridors like Massachusetts and California. This "Alpha Gap" suggests that while macro-policy sets the floor, local demand and supply constraints set the ceiling.

Ethical Interpretability & Systemic Bias

Deploying predictive models in the housing sector requires extreme caution regarding Algorithmic Redlining. We advocate for the use of SHAP values to ensure transparency and protect historically marginalized communities from purely algorithmic decision-making.

Conclusion

This semester project successfully validated the 3-month lag theory of interest rate impact. We have transitioned from raw Federal Reserve data to a high-fidelity Gradient Boosting model capable of explaining 70% of market volatility.
